Key Findings

  • SCALEAcross all 55 jurisdictions, employers have 1.37M hiring sites enrolled under 1.37M MOUs and ran 43.8M cases in FY2025. The five largest filers — Texas, California, Florida, Georgia, and New York — together account for roughly 36% of all FY2025 cases.
  • PACEFY2026 case volumes (through ~9 months) are running noticeably below an FY2025 pace in several major states even after annualizing — most visibly in Georgia, Missouri, South Carolina, and Alabama, where Activity % (sites actually verifying employees, vs. just enrolled) is also among the lowest in the country (14–19%). That combination suggests large blocks of enrolled employers in these states that are largely dormant.
  • ACTIVITYWisconsin (37.0%), Tennessee (36.3%), Iowa (35.8%) and New Hampshire (35.3%) have the highest share of enrolled sites that actively verified someone in the past year. At the other end, Northern Mariana Islands (11.5%), Georgia (14.2%), Alabama (17.0%) and Rhode Island (18.0%) have the lowest — meaning a large fraction of their enrolled hiring sites haven't used the system recently despite being signed up.
  • EFFICIENCYCases per hiring site varies enormously: Arkansas stands out at ~71 cases/site in FY2025 (versus a typical 10–25 elsewhere), suggesting either very high-turnover/seasonal hiring (e.g., agriculture, poultry processing) or a concentrated set of high-volume employers relative to its modest site count.
  • SIZE ≠ INTENSITYGeorgia has the second-most hiring sites (333K) of any state but the lowest Activity % among states (14.2%) — a much weaker activity rate than peers of similar size like California (25.4%) or Florida (33.2%), indicating Georgia's enrollment base is unusually inflated relative to ongoing use.
